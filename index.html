About Me

I am an interdisciplinary researcher who aims to enhance human learning and social relationships through technology. My work combines human-computer interaction, cognitive science, and educational psychology to develop AI-powered tools that help people understand concepts faster, master skills, and succeed in everyday life. From modeling behaviors and conducting user studies to prototyping adaptive user interfaces, I focus on one thing: developing human-centered experiences that enable autonomy and turn challenges into opportunities for growth.

Outside of work, I chase new ideas because every new experience is a lesson waiting to be learned. My latest adventure? I created this very website on GitHub (learning by doing, right?). If you're on the same wavelength, let's get together and create something remarkable together!

Work Experience

I create computer models of how people interact with robots and virtual helpers, run experiments to test them, and analyze the results to see what works best. I plan and set up these studies, keep everyone on track with clear meetings and seminars, and write and review the papers, reports, and funding proposals that share our progress. I also present our findings at conferences and workshops and guide master’s students through their own projects so they can learn and grow.

Main Projects:

MITHOS: Interactive Mixed-Reality Training for Teachers An immersive classroom simulation using virtual reality that enhance teachers' socio-emotional skills and interactive teaching capabilities.

SocialWear: Smart wearable technologies to enhance social interactions and well-being in biking.

Mindbot: An automated robot-avatar interaction model that provides emotional support for employees.

In my role as a student worker, I performed thorough QA checks on digital learning materials, verifying content accuracy, catching layout issues, and ensuring a smooth user experience. I also supported storyboarding and built and tested interactive modules.

I supported data analysis for a PhD thesis and contributed to the PIAF project to build solid classroom foundations before students start using coding tools. As part of my instructional-design work in teaching computational thinking, I developed and evaluated pen-and-paper activities, using puzzles, flowcharts, and step-by-step exercises, to teach core skills like pattern recognition, algorithm design, and problem decomposition.

Managed the waste management project (funded by OCHA) end-to-end—overseeing the design and launch of a mobile app that let users schedule recyclable pickups, receive educational reminders, and track their contributions with gamified badges to drive engagement and boost recycling rates. I also coordinated food safety projects funded by the World Food Programme (WFP) and WASH (water, sanitation, and hygiene) initiatives funded by UNICEF.

Set up emergency school shifts and trained teachers in life-skills curricula for out-of-school children. Reached 800 + learners and developed monitoring tools adopted across the NGO.
