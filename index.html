About Me

I’m an interdisciplinary researcher interested in how technology can strengthen human learning and social connection. My work blends human-computer interaction, cognitive science, and educational psychology to build AI-assisted tools that help people practise socio-emotional skills and make confident choices in everyday life. Whether I’m modelling behaviour patterns, running user studies, or prototyping adaptive interfaces, my goal is always the same: craft human-centred experiences that respect agency and turn complex challenges into opportunities for growth.

In my daily life, I’m always looking out for fresh ideas and experiences, staying curious so each experience teaches me something new. My toddler’s unfiltered perspective reminds me why empathy, resilience, and playful exploration matter.
